Advanced Portion — Game Plan

For Leo · the one section left for the Class A license

You scored 16 of 24 last time and needed 17. You missed it by a single question. This is a finish-line problem, not a start-over problem. The Advanced portion is open book, 24 questions, 60 minutes, and 17 correct to pass.

It's open book — so speed of finding answers wins

You can bring your NASCLA 11th edition and the 2018 Virginia Construction Code. The exam isn't testing memory, it's testing whether you can find the answer fast. Set the books up before test day:

  • Tab it. Use permanent tabs only (Avery or Redi-Tag). Removable Post-it tabs are not allowed and will be pulled at check-in.
  • Tab by topic, in the book's order. In NASCLA, the money topics live in: Part 2 (Estimating, Contract Management, Project Management, Safety) and Part 3 (Financial Management, Tax Basics, Lien Laws). Put a tab on the first page of each.
  • Highlight the numbers. You may highlight, underline, and index before the exam, but you cannot write in the books during the test. Pre-highlight the FICA rates, overtime rule, lien deadlines, fall-protection and trench depths, recovery-fund caps, and the markup-vs-margin formulas.
  • Index the formulas. Make a tab that says "FORMULAS" on the page with markup/margin, overhead, current ratio, and break-even so you can flip straight to it on a math question.

Pacing — 2.5 minutes per question

  • 60 minutes ÷ 24 questions = about 2 minutes 30 seconds each. The on-screen timer counts down at the top.
  • First pass: answer everything you know cold (the definitions and the topics you ace) in under a minute each. That banks time.
  • Flag and move. If a calculation or lookup is taking more than ~3 minutes, flag it, put your best guess, and come back. Never leave a question blank, a blank is a guaranteed miss and a guess is a 25% shot.
  • Use the review screen at the end to revisit flagged questions with your remaining time.
  • There's a 15-minute tutorial first that does NOT eat your 60 minutes, and a few unscored "experimental" questions may be mixed in, so don't panic if one looks unfamiliar.

Test-day checklist

  • Arrive 30 minutes early. Late = forfeited.
  • Bring two valid IDs, one a government photo ID, names matching your registration.
  • Books must be bound (no loose pages), tabbed with permanent tabs, and contain no writing added during the exam.
  • PSI provides an erasable note tablet. An on-screen calculator is typically available, confirm it in the tutorial, but bring your mental math for markup/margin just in case.
  • No phones, smartwatches, or study guides in the room.
